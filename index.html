Welcome to Mission HELIOS

Welcome aboard the journey to unveil the mysteries of Proxima Centauri b, our nearest exoplanetary neighbor. Mission HELIOS (Habitable Exoplanetary Life Inspection & Orbital Studies) is a groundbreaking venture aiming to uncover the secrets of this Earth-like world and answer the age-old question: are we alone in the universe?

World Selection: Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b orbits our closest stellar neighbor, Proxima Centauri. It lies within the habitable zone, where conditions may be right for liquid water â€” a key ingredient for life as we know it. This exoplanet is a prime candidate for our search for extraterrestrial life.

Mission Type: Orbiter and Lander

Orbiter

The orbiter will scrutinize Proxima Centauri b from above, mapping its terrain and analyzing atmospheric conditions. It will also act as a communication relay between the lander and Earth.

Lander

The lander will descend to the surface of Proxima Centauri b, conducting up-close examinations of its geology and searching for signs of water and life.

Instrumentation

Orbiter Instruments

  • Multi-wavelength Imager: Captures detailed images across various spectrums.
  • Radio Detector: Searches for electromagnetic signals and studies the planet's ionosphere.
  • Spectrograph: Analyzes atmospheric composition to detect signs of habitability.

Lander Instruments

  • Surface Sampler: Gathers and analyzes soil and rock samples.
  • Microbial Life Detector: Conducts a biosignature search in collected samples.
  • Weather Station: Monitors local weather patterns and environmental conditions.
